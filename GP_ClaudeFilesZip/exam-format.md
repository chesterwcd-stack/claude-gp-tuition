# Singapore A-Level GP Exam Format — Syllabus 8881

## Overview
- Compulsory H1 subject for all JC students
- Two papers, same day, each 50 marks (total: 100)
- Under 2026 UAS: contributes up to 10 out of 70 points
- Grade A=10pts, B=8.75, C=7.5 — the A-to-C gap of 2.5 pts affects course eligibility
- NUS Law requires minimum Grade B
- Pass (Grade E+) required for NUS, NTU, SMU admission

## Paper 1 — Essay (90 minutes, 50 marks)
- Choose 1 from 8 questions (reduced from 12 under old syllabus 8807)
- Write 500–800 word argumentative essay
- Content & Argumentation: 30 marks
- Language: 20 marks
- Questions are multi-disciplinary — topic-spotting no longer viable
- Demands clear thesis (not fence-sitting)

### Question Types
- "Discuss" — explore angles, commit to position
- "Evaluate / Assess the view" — weigh strength of a given claim
- "How far do you agree?" — take clear stance, acknowledge limits
- "Examine the claim" — test against evidence
- "To what extent" — show where on the spectrum

### Key Scope Words
- always/never = absolute claims (challenge them)
- only = exclusive (show alternatives)
- most important = ranking (compare alternatives)
- in your society = Singapore focus mandatory
- should = normative (argue what ought to be)

## Paper 2 — Comprehension (90 minutes, 50 marks)
- Based on THREE passages (up from 1-2 under old syllabus)
- Content/Skills: 35 marks | Language: 15 marks

### Components
1. Short-Answer Questions (SAQ): ~11-15 marks, primarily on Passage A
   - Literal comprehension, inference, vocabulary in context
   - Must answer in own words (paraphrasing essential)

2. Comparison Questions (NEW in 8881): 4-6 marks
   - Cross-reference ideas between passages
   - How does Passage C support/contradict Passage A or B?
   - Did not exist under old syllabus — major stumbling block

3. Summary: 8 marks
   - Based on ENTIRETY of Passage B (not selected paragraphs)
   - No opening phrase provided (students must craft own)
   - Precise paraphrasing within word limit essential

4. Application Question (AQ): 12 marks (up from 10)
   - Link passage ideas to Singapore context
   - Must engage with the passage's specific claim
   - NOT a mini-essay about Singapore — it's a RESPONSE to the passage
   - Worth 24% of Paper 2 content marks — the highest-leverage section

## Band Descriptors (Simplified)
### Content
- Band 1 (top): Assured, well-developed arguments; strong examples; clear thesis; nuanced counterarguments
- Band 2: Competent arguments; relevant examples; clear position; some opposing views
- Band 3: Some relevant arguments; may lack depth; examples generic; position unclear
- Band 4-5: Weak/missing arguments; few/no examples; no position; may misunderstand question

### Language
- Band 1: Assured use; very few errors; sophisticated vocabulary; varied structures
- Band 2: Controlled; few serious errors; varied vocabulary; meaning clear
- Band 3: Adequate; some errors; limited range
- Band 4-5: Frequent errors impede meaning; limited vocabulary
