# ARGUE Framework — Complete Reference

## Overview
ARGUE is the proprietary thinking and writing framework at the heart of the GP Mastery Programme. Every lesson, every tool, and every assessment maps back to these 5 cognitive moves.

## The 5 Letters

### A — Analyse the Question
**Tool:** Question Autopsy (4 steps)
1. Spot the Question Type: Discuss | Evaluate/Assess | How far | Examine | To what extent
2. Circle the Scope Words: always/never (absolute) | only (exclusive) | most important (ranking) | in your society (SG mandatory) | should (normative)
3. Find the Hidden Debate: What assumption does the question make? What are the two sides? Why would someone disagree?
4. Lock Your Thesis: Commit to a position. No fence-sitting. Must be arguable.

**Taught in:** Lesson 1
**Key teaching moment:** The fence-sitter intro vs clear thesis contrast (red/green comparison)

### R — Reason with Structure
**Tool:** Argument Ladder (5 rungs)
1. POINT — Topic sentence (claim for this paragraph)
2. EXPLAIN — The "because" (WHY does this support the thesis?)
3. EVIDENCE — Specific, verifiable example (passes the Google Test)
4. LINK — Explicit connection back to the question's key terms
5. ELEVATE — The "so what?" (deeper implication, broader significance) — THIS IS THE A-GRADE MOVE

**Taught in:** Lesson 2
**Key teaching moment:** Most students stop at Rung 3. Rungs 4-5 = B→A differentiator.
**Common missing rung:** EXPLAIN (students jump from claim to example without reasoning)

### G — Grapple with Counterarguments
**Tool:** 3 Counter-Moves
1. Concede & Qualify: "While it is true that [X], this is ultimately insufficient because [Y]."
2. Reframe the Debate: "The real question is not whether [X], but whether [Y]."
3. Expose the Assumption: "This argument assumes [X]. However, [evidence against X]."

**Taught in:** Lesson 3
**Key teaching moment:** Steel-man vs straw-man contrast. The "Worst Argument Competition" makes this visceral.
**Anti-pattern:** The "However sandwich" (mechanical insertion of "however" without genuine engagement)

### U — Use Evidence Strategically
**Tool:** Evidence Vault (4-Tier System)
- Tier 1 — Singapore-Specific: MANDATORY for "in your society" Qs and AQ. Policies, events, statistics.
- Tier 2 — International Case Studies: Named countries + dates. The backbone of most paragraphs.
- Tier 3 — Conceptual/Philosophical: Thinkers, frameworks, studies. Adds depth, use selectively.
- Tier 4 — Personal/Observational: Sparingly, max once per essay.

**The Google Test:** Can someone verify your example? "Denmark's wind energy" = pass. "Many countries" = fail.
**Rule:** Every body paragraph needs Tier 1 or Tier 2. Tier 3-4 are bonus, not substitutes.

**Taught in:** Lesson 4
**Key teaching moment:** The Evidence Upgrade Drill (vague → specific)

### E — Express with Precision
**Tool:** Upgrade Engine
- Vocabulary swaps (good→beneficial, bad→detrimental, shows→demonstrates)
- Sentence structure variety
- Transition upgrades (However→Yet/Nevertheless, For example→This is evident in...)
- Weekly drill: upgrade 3 words + 1 transition + 1 sentence in any paragraph

**Taught in:** Woven throughout all lessons
**Key principle:** 30 marks for content, 20 for language. Most students over-focus on the 20.

## ARGUE Applied to Paper 2
- A: Read passages strategically — identify thesis, tone, structure BEFORE answering
- R: For inference Qs, build reasoning chain from textual evidence to conclusion
- G: For comparison Qs, identify where passages agree/disagree/qualify each other
- U: For summary, select most relevant points, paraphrase precisely within word limits
- E: For AQ, craft response demonstrating genuine engagement with passage in SG context

## Supporting Frameworks (Layered Beneath ARGUE)
- Paul-Elder Critical Thinking: simplified as "The 5 Checks" (clear? accurate? deep? relevant? fair?)
- De Bono Thinking Hats: adapted as "The Lens Shift" (Economic, Ethical, Practical, Cultural, Scientific, Political lenses)
- Toulmin's Model: simplified as Claim + Grounds + Warrant + Backing + Qualifier + Rebuttal
