# Lesson Structure Template — SPARK-TEACH-PRACTISE-PROVE-REFLECT

## Session Format
- Duration: 2 hours (120 minutes)
- Group size: 4–6 students
- Frequency: 1 session per week
- Total Phase 1: 12 sessions (3 months)

## The 5 Segments

### SPARK (0:00–0:15) — 15 minutes
**Purpose:** Warm-up. Get brains firing. Build current affairs reflexes.
**Typical activities:**
- Headline Roulette (draw headline, 60 sec to name theme + thesis)
- Quick Debate (provocative statement, 60-second takes)
- Think Card review (students share one insight from homework reading)
- Question Autopsy speed drill (decode a new GP question)
- News Flash (discuss a headline from the week)
- XP challenge review from previous week

**Design principle:** Fast-paced, low-stakes, high-energy. Sets the tone for the session.

### TEACH (0:15–0:45) — 30 minutes
**Purpose:** Core instruction. New framework, technique, or content.
**Key principles:**
- Interactive and Socratic — ask, don't lecture
- Tutor MODELS the thinking process live, not just the output
- Students participate, predict, and discover rather than passively receive
- Use the whiteboard to build ideas visually in real time
- Never read from the Student Handout in class — teach from the Tutor Guide

**Design principle:** The tutor is a thinking coach, not a lecturer. Every 5 minutes should include a student response point.

### PRACTISE (0:45–1:15) — 30 minutes
**Purpose:** Guided practice with coaching.
**Typical activities:**
- Pair/group work applying the lesson's concept
- Writing paragraphs with real-time tutor feedback
- Debate activities (Devil's Advocate Chair)
- Argument building on whiteboards
- Comprehension exercises in pairs
- Evidence Wars, Argument Auction, Lens Shift

**Design principle:** Students do the work. Tutor circulates, coaches, corrects in real time. Mistakes are learning opportunities, not failures.

### PROVE (1:15–1:45) — 30 minutes
**Purpose:** Independent application under exam-like conditions.
**Typical tasks:**
- Solo timed writing (paragraph, introduction, or full essay)
- Comprehension practice set
- AQ response
- Speed Thesis drill

**Design principle:** This is where the rubber meets the road. Strict timing. Collected for marking. Produces progress data.

### REFLECT (1:45–2:00) — 15 minutes
**Purpose:** Debrief, consolidate, preview, motivate.
**Standard flow:**
1. One takeaway per student (what will you remember?)
2. Preview next week's topic
3. Homework assignment (specific, time-estimated)
4. XP awards and leaderboard update
5. Closing motivation

**Design principle:** Students leave knowing exactly what to do before next session and feeling positive about their progress.

## Homework Rotation (4-week cycle)
| Week | Type | Duration | Skill |
|------|------|----------|-------|
| 1 | Full essay (timed) | 90 min | Integrated writing |
| 2 | Paragraph upgrade drill | 30 min | Argumentation depth |
| 3 | Paper 2 practice set | 60 min | Comprehension |
| 4 | Evidence Vault expansion | 30 min | Content knowledge |

Plus: Think Card EVERY week (~15 min)

## Phase 1: IGNITE — 12-Lesson Arc

### Month 1: THE ARGUE TOOLKIT (L1-4)
- L1: "Why GP Is Not English" — A (Analyse / Question Autopsy)
- L2: "Building Arguments That Convince" — R (Reason / Argument Ladder)
- L3: "The Art of the Counterargument" — G (Grapple / 3 Counter-Moves)
- L4: "The Evidence Vault" — U (Use Evidence / 4-Tier System) + FIRST FULL ESSAY

### Month 2: THEME DEEP-DIVES (L5-8)
- L5: "Science, Tech & The Future" — CP01 content + Lens Shift exercise
- L6: "Green Planet, Grey Choices" — CP02 content + Fusion Essay Planner
- L7: "People, Power & Inequality" — CP03 content + Perspective Grid
- L8: "Boss Battle" — Full timed essay challenge (60 min)

### Month 3: EXAM SHARPENING (L9-12)
- L9: "Cracking Paper 2" — 3-Pass reading method + SAQ technique
- L10: "Comparison & Summary" — Cross-Reference Method + SCAN method
- L11: "The AQ Goldmine" — AQ Architecture + SG application
- L12: "Phase 1 Finale" — Full exam simulation (both papers)

## Game Formats Library
- Headline Roulette: Random headlines, 60-sec theme + thesis
- Argument Auction: Bid on arguments, evaluate quality
- Boss Battle (Monthly): Unseen question, team planning, class vote
- Evidence Wars: Head-to-head, each provides better example
- The Rewrite: Fix a deliberately bad AQ response
- Speed Thesis: 8 questions, 30 seconds each, one-sentence thesis
- Devil's Advocate Chair: Argue FOR then AGAINST, switch roles
- Worst Argument Competition: Write weakest counterargument (teaches straw-man vs steel-man)
- The Lens Shift: Same question through Economic/Ethical/Practical/Cultural/Scientific/Political lenses
- Perspective Grid: Matrix of groups × dimensions for "in your society" questions

## Feedback Model: GLOW-GROW-GO
- GLOW: What the student did well (specific, evidence-based praise)
- GROW: The SINGLE most important thing to improve (not a laundry list)
- GO: A specific, actionable next step
Deadline: Within 72 hours of collection.
